# Example Client DNA Profile

Illustrative example of a finished profile. Content is fictional — structure matches real output.

```markdown
# Client DNA — Noa Levy Coaching
## Product: 90-Day Business Clarity Program (group coaching for early-stage service providers)

---

## IDEAL CLIENT SNAPSHOT

She's 28-35, left a corporate job 6-18 months ago to start her own thing — coaching, consulting, design, copywriting, something service-based. She's getting some clients through word of mouth and Instagram, but there's no system. Every month feels like starting from zero. She's not broke, but she's burning through savings and starting to wonder if she made a mistake leaving her job.

---

## THE WORLD BEFORE (Current State)

### Core Problems
- **Problem:** Can't predict where the next client is coming from
  - **Cost:** Constant financial anxiety, can't plan more than a month ahead
  - **Duration:** Since she started — 6-18 months of this cycle
- **Problem:** Posting on Instagram daily but it's not converting to paying clients
  - **Cost:** 2-3 hours a day creating content that generates likes but not revenue
  - **Duration:** Since the beginning — she was told "just post consistently"
- **Problem:** Doesn't know how to price or position her services
  - **Cost:** Undercharging, overdelivering, attracting price-sensitive clients who drain her
  - **Duration:** Has never felt confident about her pricing

### Mistakes They're Making
- Trying to appeal to everyone instead of choosing a niche (unaware — she thinks broad = more opportunities)
- Spending 80% of her time on content creation and 20% on actual business building (somewhat aware — feels it but doesn't know the alternative)
- Copying what bigger accounts do without understanding the strategy behind it (unaware — thinks the format IS the strategy)

### What They've Already Tried
- **Free content and courses online** — gave her frameworks but not accountability or personalized guidance. She has 47 saved Reels about "how to get clients" and none of them changed anything.
- **A cheap online course ($200-500)** — too generic, felt like she was being talked at, not helped. Finished 40% of it.
- **A business coach (1:1, expensive)** — good but unsustainable at her income level. Couldn't justify the cost when revenue was inconsistent.
- **Residual effect:** Skeptical of programs that promise results. Has a folder of screenshots of promises from courses that didn't deliver. But still looking — because the pain hasn't gone away.

### The False Belief
- **What they think they need:** A better content strategy. More followers. A viral Reel. A prettier brand.
- **Why it's wrong/incomplete:** Content is a distribution channel, not a business model. Without clear positioning, a defined offer, and a simple sales process, more followers just means more people who don't buy.
- **What they actually need:** Clarity on who they serve, a structured offer at the right price point, and a repeatable way to turn conversations into clients — before optimizing content.

---

## THE WORLD AFTER (Desired State)

### Practical Transformation
- Has 3-5 discovery calls booked per week from a simple, repeatable system
- Raised her prices by 40-60% and stopped getting pushback
- Spends 30 minutes a day on content instead of 3 hours — and it converts better
- **Before vs. After:** Before — wakes up, checks Instagram analytics anxiously, spends the morning creating a post, gets 43 likes and zero DMs. After — wakes up, has two inquiry messages from a lead magnet that runs on autopilot, responds with her standard qualifying questions, books one call before lunch.

### Emotional Transformation
- Stops introducing herself apologetically ("I'm kind of a coach, I guess")
- Feels like she has a real business, not a side project she's hoping works out
- Stops comparing herself to accounts with 50K followers and feeling behind
- **The testimonial moment:** "I just signed two clients this week at my new price and neither of them blinked. I literally cried in my car after the call. This is actually working."

### Identity Shift
"I used to be someone who left her job and was secretly terrified she'd have to go back. Now I'm someone who runs a business that pays her bills and actually has a waitlist."

---

## BUYING BARRIERS

### Self-Directed ("Not for me because...")
- "I've already invested in courses and coaching — I should be able to figure this out myself by now" (shame about not having figured it out yet)
- "I'm not at the level where I need a program — I need to get more clients first, then invest" (the chicken-and-egg trap)

### Solution-Directed ("This won't work because...")
- "Group programs are generic — my situation is different/more complicated"
- "90 days isn't enough to build a real business"
- **Comparison in their head:** A cheaper self-paced course ($97-297) that promises the same outcomes. Or free content from a bigger creator who seems to give everything away.

### Timing ("Not now because...")
- "I need to make more money first before I can invest in this"
- "I'll join the next cohort — I need to finish [other course/project] first"
- **Trigger moment:** She loses a client she was counting on. Or she sees someone with half her talent and a quarter of her experience signing clients confidently. Or her savings hit a number that scares her. Or a friend asks "so how's the business going?" and she doesn't know what to say.

---

## CLIENT LANGUAGE BANK

### How They Describe Their Problem
- "I feel like I'm doing everything right and nothing is working"
- "I don't know what I'm doing wrong — I'm posting every day"
- "I know I'm good at what I do, I just can't figure out the business side"
- "I'm tired of hearing 'just be consistent' — I AM consistent"
- "Everyone else seems to have figured this out except me"

### How They Describe What They Want
- "I just want a system that works — something I can follow"
- "I want to know exactly what to do every day without second-guessing"
- "I want clients who value what I do and don't haggle on price"

### Emotional Vocabulary
- **Frustration words:** stuck, spinning my wheels, drowning, lost, exhausted, burnt out, invisible
- **Aspiration words:** freedom, clarity, flow, confidence, booked out, in demand
- **Metaphors they reach for:** "like shouting into the void," "hamster wheel," "throwing spaghetti at the wall," "building the plane while flying it"

### Search Behavior
- **Google searches:** "how to get clients as a freelancer," "why isn't my Instagram growing," "how to price coaching services"
- **YouTube searches:** "client attraction strategy for coaches," "Instagram strategy for small business 2024"
- **Social media:** Engages with "day in my life as a business owner" content, before/after transformations, behind-the-scenes of successful freelancers

---

## AWARENESS LEVEL

- **Problem awareness:** Fully aware — she knows something isn't working
- **Solution awareness:** Somewhat aware — knows coaching/programs exist but skeptical they'll work for her
- **Product awareness:** Low — doesn't know this specific program exists
- **Content implication:** Lead with pain validation ("you're not doing it wrong — you're missing one piece"), then introduce the mechanism

---

## THE FALSE BELIEF FRAMEWORK

- **Surface problem:** "I can't get consistent clients"
- **False belief:** "I need better content / more followers / a nicer brand"
- **Why it fails:** Content is amplification — if the underlying offer and positioning are unclear, better content just amplifies confusion faster. She's optimizing distribution before she has something clear to distribute.
- **True mechanism:** Positioning-first approach — define who you serve, build one clear offer, create a simple conversion path. Content becomes the last step, not the first.
- **Proof:** Clients in the program raise prices within 3 weeks and book calls within 6 weeks — before changing anything about their content strategy.

---

## POSITIONING SUMMARY

She's a talented service provider, 6-18 months into her business, doing all the "right things" — posting daily, showing up, being consistent — and it's not converting. She's tried courses, consumed every free resource, maybe hired a coach she couldn't sustain. She believes she needs better content or more followers. She doesn't. She needs to stop building on a foundation that doesn't exist yet: clear positioning, a defined offer, and a simple path from stranger to client. When that's in place, the content she's already creating starts working — and she goes from anxious freelancer to booked-out business owner who trusts her own system.

---

## READY-TO-USE CONTEXT BLOCK

You are writing for Noa Levy Coaching, targeting early-stage female service providers (coaches, consultants, freelancers) who are 6-18 months into their business. When she gets this right, she books 3-5 calls per week, raises her prices without pushback, and finally feels like she's running a real business. The biggest barrier is skepticism from past investments that didn't deliver and shame about not having figured this out yet. Use her language: "spinning my wheels," "doing everything right and nothing's working," "I know I'm good at what I do," "throwing spaghetti at the wall." Lead with pain validation — she IS doing the work, she's just building in the wrong order.
```
