# Client DNA Profile — Output Template

After completing the interactive conversation, synthesize all answers into this format. This is the deliverable — a structured document that any LLM can use to generate targeted marketing content.

```markdown
# Client DNA — [Business/Brand Name]
## Product: [Specific Product or Service]

---

## IDEAL CLIENT SNAPSHOT

3-4 sentences max. A briefing for a copywriter on day one. Who is this person, what's
their situation, and what are they looking for? Written as if describing a real person,
not a market segment.

---

## THE WORLD BEFORE (Current State)

### Core Problems
For each problem (2-4):
- **Problem:** [What hurts — in the client's language]
- **Cost:** [What it costs them — time, money, energy, relationships]
- **Duration:** [How long they've dealt with this]

### Mistakes They're Making
- [Mistake 1 — and whether they're aware of it]
- [Mistake 2]
- [Mistake 3]

### What They've Already Tried
- [Failed solution 1 — why it didn't work]
- [Failed solution 2 — why it didn't work]
- [Failed solution 3 — why it didn't work]
- **Residual effect:** [How these failures affect their willingness to try again]

### The False Belief
- **What they think they need:** [Their assumed solution]
- **Why it's wrong/incomplete:** [The gap in their thinking]
- **What they actually need:** [The real solution, per the business's approach]

---

## THE WORLD AFTER (Desired State)

### Practical Transformation
- [Concrete change 1]
- [Concrete change 2]
- [Concrete change 3]
- **Before vs. After:** [A specific day-in-the-life contrast]

### Emotional Transformation
- [Feeling shift 1]
- [Feeling shift 2]
- [What they stop worrying about]
- **The testimonial moment:** [The message a happy client actually sends]

### Identity Shift
"I used to be someone who [old identity]. Now I'm someone who [new identity]."

---

## BUYING BARRIERS

### Self-Directed ("Not for me because...")
- [Objection 1 — the real reason, not the polite one]
- [Objection 2]

### Solution-Directed ("This won't work because...")
- [Doubt 1]
- [Doubt 2]
- **Comparison in their head:** [What competitor or alternative they're weighing]

### Timing ("Not now because...")
- [Delay reason 1]
- [Delay reason 2]
- **Trigger moment:** [What finally makes them act — the specific event or realization]

---

## CLIENT LANGUAGE BANK

### How They Describe Their Problem
Quoted phrases — pulled directly from DMs, intake forms, calls, comments:
- "[Exact phrase 1]"
- "[Exact phrase 2]"
- "[Exact phrase 3]"
- "[Exact phrase 4]"

### How They Describe What They Want
- "[Exact phrase 1]"
- "[Exact phrase 2]"
- "[Exact phrase 3]"

### Emotional Vocabulary
- **Frustration words:** [words they use when stuck/angry]
- **Aspiration words:** [words they use when dreaming/hoping]
- **Metaphors they reach for:** [comparisons they naturally make]

### Search Behavior
- **Google searches:** [What they'd type]
- **YouTube searches:** [What they'd look for]
- **Social media:** [What content they engage with]

---

## AWARENESS LEVEL

- **Problem awareness:** [Fully aware / Somewhat aware / Unaware]
- **Solution awareness:** [Knows solutions exist / Doesn't know this type of solution exists]
- **Product awareness:** [Knows about this business / Doesn't know it exists]
- **Content implication:** [Lead with pain / Lead with solution / Lead with differentiation]

---

## THE FALSE BELIEF FRAMEWORK

This is the single most important positioning element. It structures all persuasive content.

- **Surface problem:** [What the client says is wrong]
- **False belief:** [What they think will fix it]
- **Why it fails:** [Why that belief leads them astray]
- **True mechanism:** [What actually fixes it — the business's unique approach]
- **Proof:** [Evidence this works — results, stories, data]

---

## POSITIONING SUMMARY

~100 words. A dense paragraph that captures: who the ideal client is, what they're
struggling with, what they've tried, what they believe that's wrong, what actually
works, and what changes when it does. This paragraph should make the ideal client
feel seen and make the non-ideal client self-select out.

---

## READY-TO-USE CONTEXT BLOCK

~200 words, pasteable into any AI system prompt alongside a Voice DNA Profile.
Encodes: who the audience is, their core pain, their language, their false belief,
the real solution, the transformation, and the primary objection to address.

This block should be written as instructions to an AI:

"You are writing for [business] targeting [ideal client]. This person is dealing
with [core problems] and has tried [failed solutions]. They believe [false belief]
but what they actually need is [true mechanism]. When they succeed, [transformation].
The biggest barrier is [primary objection]. Use their language: [key phrases].
Always lead with [awareness-appropriate hook]."
```
